# Case Study – UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## Type of Cybercrime
**UPI and Online Payment Fraud** (a form of Financial Cybercrime)

---

## Case Study (200–250 words)

### Overview
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India. It targets individuals of all backgrounds, but college students — who frequently use apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, and Paytm for everyday transactions — are increasingly at risk.

### How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **Initial contact:** The fraudster contacts the victim via phone, WhatsApp, or social media, posing as a customer support representative, a bank official, or a fellow student selling something online (e.g., on OLX or Instagram Marketplace).
2. **Building trust:** The fraudster uses official-sounding language, may share a fake employee ID, and appears genuinely helpful.
3. **The trap:** The victim is asked to "verify" their account, "receive a refund," or "complete a transaction" by scanning a QR code or entering a UPI PIN.
4. **The fraud:** Once the victim enters their PIN or scans the code, money is immediately debited from their account — not credited. UPI PINs are only ever needed to *send* money, never to receive it.
5. **Disappearance:** The fraudster immediately blocks the victim and transfers funds to mule accounts.

### Who Is Targeted
College students buying or selling second-hand items, people expecting online refunds, and anyone unfamiliar with how UPI PIN authorisation actually works.

### Consequences
Victims lose money instantly and recovery is difficult. Psychological distress, embarrassment, and financial hardship are common, especially for students on limited budgets. In India, many cases go unreported due to fear of embarrassment.
