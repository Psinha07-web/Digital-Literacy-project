# 🔒 Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
**For College Students in India**

---

## ✅ 8 Actionable Prevention Tips

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** — Never reuse the same password across multiple apps or websites. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Consider a password manager like Bitwarden.

2. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** — Turn on 2FA on all important accounts (email, Instagram, banking apps). Even if your password is stolen, your account stays protected.

3. **Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone** — No bank, UPI app (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm), or government body will ever call or text asking for your PIN or OTP. If someone does — it's a scam, hang up immediately.

4. **Be alert to "collect money" UPI requests** — Scammers send fake UPI payment requests disguised as money transfers. Entering your PIN on a *collect request* means you are SENDING money, not receiving it. Always read the screen carefully before confirming.

5. **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks** — Never log into banking apps, UPI, or college portals on café or hostel public Wi-Fi. Use your mobile data or a trusted VPN instead.

6. **Think before you click — beware of phishing links** — Fake links in WhatsApp forwards, emails, or SMS often look real (e.g., "sbi-kyc-update.com"). Always verify the URL and never enter credentials on unfamiliar websites.

7. **Audit your social media privacy settings regularly** — Check what information is publicly visible on Instagram, Snapchat, and LinkedIn. Avoid posting your phone number, location, or daily routine publicly.

8. **Download apps only from official stores** — Never install APK files sent via WhatsApp or unknown websites. Unofficial apps can contain spyware that silently steals your data.

## 🆘 If You Are a Victim — Report Immediately

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| 🌐 National Cyber Crime Portal | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) |
| 📞 Cyber Crime Helpline | **1930** (Toll-free, 24x7) |
| 🏫 College IT Cell | Report through your institution if college accounts are compromised |

> **Act fast** — In cases of financial fraud, calling 1930 within the first few hours significantly increases the chance of recovering your money.
